    Player Profile

    Shannon Szabados

    Shannon Szabados aims to complete the hat trick of Olympic gold medals, after Team Canada's defeat during the 2018 Winter Olympics in PyongChang. Szabados, one of the most recognizable goaltenders in world, aims to get her third gold when Team Canada goes back to the 2022 Olympics. Until then, she will continue to play for the NWHL's Buffalo beauts. During the NWHL's 4th All Star Game, Szabados was named one of the team captains along with Lee Stecklein. The future is bright for the NWHL with Szabados in net.
    Favorite Player Growing Up: "Angela James, and Patrick Roy."
    Favorite Memory:"My first Olympic Gold, standing up on the podium."
    Interesting Mask Fact: "I have 'FLM' on my mask to remind to 'Fight Like Matt', a sledge hockey player I know."

    David Aebischer

    Being called up to be a backup for the first time is always a nerve racking experience, but never moreso when the goalie you're backing up is the legendary Patrick Roy. This is exactly what happened to David Aebischer in 2000 when the Avalanche traded Marc Denis to the Blue Jackets. Aebischer enjoyed a good career, even starting in the 2004 playoffs for Colorado, before being traded to montreal for Joe Theodore. He would eventually bounce around between Montreal, Phoenix, and Winnipeg, before going to play overseasHC Thurgau..

    Marty Turco

    Marty Turco was the guy in net for the Dallas Stars for a number of years. After Modano, when you thought of Dallas hockey, you thought Turco. After taking over the net in 2001 when Ed Belfour decided to sign in Toronto, Turco's GAA of 1.72 set a modern day record, and even had 3 shutouts in 7 playoff games, unfortunately losing the series after his teammates couldn't produce enough goal scoring opportunities. After leaving Dallas, Turco signed with Chicago to take over for the departing Antii Niemi, and later signed as second backup with the Bruins.
    Favorite Player Growing Up: "Hasek, Dominator for sure."
    Favorite Memory:"Winning the Stanley Cup."
    Interesting Mask Fact: "I love the gargoyles on my mask.

    The Other Masks

    One of the more interesting things you might notice with the infamous Detroit-Colorado rivalries of the 90s/00s was the unique goalie mask that both Osgood and Hasek wore. Or maybe when Tim Thomas was winning the Stanley Cup in 2011 you may have seen him take a puck of his player helmet with a non-cat's eye goalie cage. Well, after exhaustive study and researcg, which totally wasn't just calling up each player and asking about it, we've found that these goalies use them not because they feel any safer wearing them, but because of comfort. Osgood finds they feel more comfortable than the traditional goalie mask, while Hasek and Thomas say they've been using the same mask style since they were kids, and they just prefer them.

    Tuukka Rask

    Can you imagine a world where Tuukka Rask is wearing Maple Leaf blue? Well, it almost happened, when Rask was drafted by Toronto 21st overall in the 2005 NHL entry draft. His rights were traded to Boston in exchange for Andrew Raycroft. Since then, Rask has backstopped the Bruins to two Stanley Cup appearances, whiel Raycroft has, uh, not. In 2019, Rask passed Tiny Thompson's franchise record of games started by a goalie in Bruin's history with his 469th game. Internationally, the Finnish Rask helped Team Finland to two Bronze medals.
    Favorite Player Growing Up: "Kiprusoff, for sure. Toskala also."
    Favorite Memory:"Celebrating and lifting the Stanley Cup."
    Interesting Mask Fact: "I've had pretty much the same Bruin on my mask since I was signed. His name's Barry."

    Why Goalies Choose Their Art

    The question is simple, but the answers weren't: Why do goalies get whay they do painted on their masks? Basically, after talking to several net minders, the answers were incredibly varied. The majority of them are personal, putting family on the sides, or their beliefs. It's not uncommon to see scripture that spoke to them referenced on the mask. However, the higher in professional leagues you get, the more say the team has, often times nixing what the goalie wants. For example, Montreal has often told Carey Price he can't have more cowboy references on his masks, making them more "team-centric". Same for the late Ray Emery, who had the infamous Mike Tyson on his mask for a short while, before the Flyers asked him to take it off. Typically, the masks at the NHL level are almost entirely at least approved before even being painted, though how much teams care can vary from team to team. Roberto Luongo had an incredibly complicated and detailed mask during his time in Vancouver, however when he went back to Florida, he was allowed to go a lot more simple, reigning in the design to show off a basic panther symbol and gold stripes.
